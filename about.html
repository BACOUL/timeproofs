Why now

Information is fluid. AI systems rewrite content in milliseconds, creators iterate continuously, and critical decisions stream through software. Proving that “something existed at T” is now essential for trust, attribution, audit, and compliance. Traditional options are heavy (blockchains, gas, delays), closed (silos), or intrusive (uploading your files). Teams either don’t timestamp—or do it too late.

What TimeProofs does

1) Hash locally
Compute a SHA-256 of any content: AI output, file, email, webhook, dataset.
2) Timestamp via API
POST the hash to /timestamp. We return an ISO time and an HMAC signature.
3) Verify publicly
Anyone can call /verify?hash=… to confirm existence + timestamp + signature.

No data leaves your device Edge-native (Cloudflare) Open & simple

FAQ (short)

Why not a blockchain?

We optimize for instant proofs, low cost, and privacy by default—no gas fees, no confirmation delays, no token.

Do you store my files?

No. We only handle the SHA-256 hash. Your content never leaves your device.

Is it legally useful?

It’s strong technical evidence of existence/integrity at time T. Legal weight depends on jurisdiction/context.

Can anyone verify?

Yes. The /verify endpoint is public, read-only, and idempotent.
