Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

Version: v0.1-draft · Effective: 2025-10-18 · Last updated: 2025-10-18

This DPA forms part of the Terms and applies to processing of personal data under GDPR/UK GDPR and comparable laws.

1) Roles and scope

  • Customer is the controller.
  • TimeProofs is the processor for in-scope personal data.
  • Design note: The Service is hash-only. Do not send personal data in meta. If Customer includes personal data in meta despite guidance, this DPA governs such processing.

2) Instructions

TimeProofs processes personal data solely:

  • to provide timestamp and verification services as documented;
  • per written lawful instructions from Customer; and
  • to comply with law, informing Customer unless prohibited.

3) Processing details

Subject matterProvision of digital proof-of-existence services.
DurationTerm of the Agreement. Proofs retained per Privacy Policy.
NatureStoring SHA-256 hash, timestamp, signature, optional small meta; public verification by hash.
PurposeIntegrity, existence attestation, abuse prevention, reliability.
Data categoriesBy design none. If Customer includes personal data in meta, categories are determined by Customer.
Data subjectsDetermined by Customer.

4) Security

  • TLS in transit; HMAC-SHA256 server signatures; key rotation.
  • Access controls, least privilege, logging, rate limiting, time sync.
  • Minimal data design; no original content upload.

See also Privacy and security.txt.

5) Sub-processors

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — Workers, KV, network security, routing.

TimeProofs ensures written data protection terms with sub-processors and remains liable for their acts/omissions. Changes will be posted on this page or Legal. Customer may object on reasonable grounds; if unresolved, Customer may terminate affected services.

6) Assistance

  • TimeProofs assists Customer in responding to data subject requests, security, DPIAs, and consultations, considering the hash-only model.
  • Customer is responsible for verifying the specific hash related to a request.

7) Incidents

TimeProofs notifies Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting Customer data. Notice includes available details, remediation, and contact.

8) Return and deletion

Upon termination or on written request, TimeProofs deletes personal data processed for Customer, unless retention is required by law or necessary for the integrity of the public verification service. Deleting a proof disables public verifiability for that hash.

9) Audits

On request once per 12 months, TimeProofs makes available summaries of security measures and third-party reports (if any). Customer may conduct a targeted audit with reasonable notice, during business hours, without disrupting services. Mutual NDAs apply.

10) International transfers

TimeProofs uses global edge infrastructure. Where transfer of personal data outside the EEA/UK occurs, the parties adopt the applicable EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and UK Addendum by reference. TimeProofs implements supplementary measures consistent with the hash-only architecture.

11) Records and cooperation

TimeProofs maintains processing records and cooperates with competent supervisory authorities upon lawful request.

12) Liability

Liability limits and exclusions in the Terms apply to this DPA. Nothing excludes liability where prohibited by law.

13) Miscellaneous

  • Inconsistency: this DPA prevails over the Terms to the extent of conflict on data protection.
  • Changes: TimeProofs may update this DPA to reflect legal or service changes. Material updates will be notified.
  • Governing law: as in the Terms, without prejudice to mandatory data protection laws.

Signatures

This DPA is deemed executed when the Customer accepts the Terms or signs an Order Form referencing this DPA.
