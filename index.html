Purpose

Why it’s new — and why it matters

Proof systems today are closed, centralized, or blockchain-based. TimeProofs introduces an open, privacy-first protocol that certifies when something existed and that it hasn’t changed — without storing or revealing it. Think of it as HTTPS for authenticity.

Protocol details

Technical Layer — Inside the Proof

TimeProofs uses deterministic hashing (SHA-256) and HMAC-signed timestamps. Proofs are verifiable via any ProofSpec-compliant client, online or offline.
